The Green Revolution is the term given to the use of high-yielding varieties (HYVs) of wheat and rice particularly during the 1960s to increase food crop production, especially in India. The new seed varieties, popularly called miracle, seeds, were developed in Mexico (wheat) and the Philippines (rice), but it was the new dwarf varieties of wheat which provided the bigger growth
